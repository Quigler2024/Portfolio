Video Projects

I have developed strong video editing skills through extensive hands-on experience using Adobe Premiere Pro, which I use as my primary editing software for both academic and personal projects. My editing approach is grounded in narrative storytelling, where careful attention is given to pacing, structure, and visual information to ensure each video communicates its message clearly and effectively to the audience. I also place a strong emphasis on sound design,working with dialogue, music, and ambient audio to create balance and enhance emotional impact, often drawing inspiration from other movies and cinematic audio techniques.


Film always been a significant influence on my creative process, shaping how I cut scenes, build tension, and guide the viewer’s focus through intentional sequencing and timing. While I recognise that there is still a lot for me to learn, I am highly motivated to continue developing my skills, experimenting with new techniques, and refining my creative thinking when it comes to moving images. I am eager to grow as an editor and constantly challenge myself to improve, using each project as a new opportunity to become more confident and technically proficient.

DCU

Short Film Project

"Seen Not Heard" is a short film project I created that highlights the challenges deaf students faced during the pandemic due to the widespread use of masks. The film sheds light on the barriers deaf individuals encounter daily and how they navigate a hearing world. With the implementation of COVID-19 masks, an additional challenge arises: lip-reading others becomes even more difficult.

Video Production Module

Video Clip 1 - Favourite Movie

This one-minute video was created as part of a group assignment exploring visual storytelling through personal narrative. Working in a group of fifteen, we filmed short interviews with participants discussing their favourite movies and why they resonated with them. I chose to focus on one participant whose vivid visual descriptions allowed for stronger storytelling. This approach enabled me to incorporate third-party material and audio to enhance the overall narrative and emotional impact.

Video Clip 2 - Short Film Project

This short film follows a student who is rejected from Trinity College, with the decision tied to deeper personal issues relating to his father. As he struggles to understand the rejection, the story explores themes of pressure, identity, and emotional conflict. The character becomes increasingly consumed by the need for answers, leading him to take matters into his own hands. The film uses pacing, sound, and visual tension to reflect his gradual loss of control and internal turmoil.

Personal Video Work

Video Clip 1 – Belgium Trip Montage

This video is a three-minute montage capturing my travels through Belgium, highlighting cities such as Ghent, Bruges, and Dinant. The video combines scenic shots, landmarks, and personal experiences to convey the journey and atmosphere of each location. By sequencing the clips with music and transitions, I aimed to create a sense of exploration and immersion through sharing my personal perspective of the trip.

Video Clip 2 – Human Eye Explanation

This was my first ever personal video project, focusing on the repetition and experience of what our eyes capture. While the video was a learning experience and not as polished as I would make it now, it allowed me to experiment with explanatory storytelling, visuals, and pacing. Reflecting on it, I recognise ways I could improve the editing and clarity, but it remains an important milestone as my first attempt at creating a personal video.

Social Media Videos

I have also created social media video content using both Adobe Premiere Pro and CapCut, adapting my editing style to suit fast-paced, platform-specific formats. These personal/academic projects required concise informative narration, strong visual hooks, and an understanding of how pacing and timing impact viewer engagement across platforms. Through this work, I gained experience editing for quick pace, vertical, and horizontal content, ensuring videos remained visually engaging while simultaneously communicating the message through a limited timeframe.
