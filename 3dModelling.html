3D Modelling

I have developed strong 3D modelling skills through extensive hands-on experience using Blender, Meshroom, and Sketchfab, which I use as my primary tools for both academic and personal projects. My approach is grounded in combining technical precision with creative exploration, carefully considering topology, lighting, texturing, and rendering to ensure each model communicates its intended form and function effectively. I also place a strong emphasis on photogrammetry and real-world reference integration, often using Meshroom to capture accurate environments and Sketchfab to present interactive, web-ready 3D models for immersive visualisation.

Object One: Iron


My first 3D modelling project involved creating an iron using 3dx MAX, focusing on basic geometric construction and the application of spline pipe techniques. I combined a triangle, rectangle, and spline-based pipe shapes to construct the overall form, carefully considering proportions, alignment, and curvature to achieve a clean and realistic silhouette.

This assignment allowed me to understand the fundamentals of 3D modelling, including mesh creation, object manipulation, and precise shape integration. It served as an essential exercise in developing spatial awareness, control over 3dx Max tools, and understanding how simple geometric forms can be combined to create recognizable, functional objects.

Object Two: Cube / Sphere intersection


This project focused on exploring object intersection and subtraction techniques in Blender. Using a cube and a sphere, I experimented with Boolean operations to subtract and combine shapes, learning how different geometries interact in 3D space.

The exercise allowed me to develop a stronger understanding of mesh topology, precise alignment, and clean geometry, while also improving my skills in manipulating complex forms. This foundational practice helped reinforce the principles of constructive and destructive modelling, which are essential for creating more intricate 3D objects.

3D Modelling: Room Assignment

This project involved creating a fully realized 3D TV room scene in Blender, incorporating all required key elements: a transmissive/refractive object (glass), a highly reflective surface, a bump-mapped/normal-mapped object, a texture-mapped object, carefully positioned lights and shadows, and a surrounding 3D environment. I carefully designed and arranged each element to create a cohesive, realistic interior while balancing visual interest and spatial composition.

The room was also required to have at least five pop culture toy objects made from scratch, which were the Pac-Man arcade set, the Pokémon ball, the Nintendo Playboy, the Rubik’s Cube, the Nintendo Switch, and the teddy bear.

This project allowed me to experiment with advanced 3D techniques such as lighting design, material creation, texture mapping, reflections, refractions, and environmental composition. It strengthened my ability to combine multiple 3D elements into a cohesive, immersive interior, while balancing realism and visual storytelling within a controlled scene.

The placement of objects in the scene was carefully considered to create a balanced and visually engaging composition.

-Larger items, such as the Pac-Man arcade set (in the corner of the room) and the Teddy bear(on the sofa) were positioned in different parts of the room to anchor the scene and establish realistic scale.

-Mid-sized objects, like the Nintendo Switch and Pokémon ball, were arranged on surfaces and tables to guide the viewer's eye naturally across the room.

-Smaller objects, including the Rubriks cube and the Ninetedo playboy, were placed strategically to add detail, interest, and a sense of playfulness without cluttering the scene.

The bookshelves in the room were carefully designed to appear realistic and lived-in. They are filled with books and miscellaneous items, such as storage boxes, a small alarm clock and plants, arranged at slightly varied angles to avoid uniformity and create a natural, organic look. Some books lean against others, while a few are stacked horizontally to break monotony, adding subtle visual interest.

The shelves were positioned to complement the room's layout, aligning with walls and light sources to enhance depth and perspective. Shadows cast by the books and objects reinforce their three-dimensionality, while subtle rotations and offsets give the impression that the room has been used, making the scene feel more immersive and believable.

The Pokémon Ball was modelled entirely from scratch using Blender. I began by creating a perfect sphere as the base and then carefully divided it into two hemispheres to replicate the iconic red and white design. A thin black ring was added around the equator to separate the halves, and a small circular button was created on the front to match the classic Pokémon Ball features.

The ceramic vase and cups were placed on a circular marble table to introduce contrast in both material and form. The smooth, slightly glossy ceramic surfaces were designed to reflect light subtly, differentiating them from the harder, more reflective marble tabletop. This contrast helps emphasize material realism and adds visual depth to the scene.

Their placement was carefully considered to feel natural and functional rather than staged. The vase acts as a central anchor on the table, while the cups are positioned with slight spacing and variation in rotation to suggest everyday use. Subtle shadows and reflections were used to ground the objects on the marble surface, reinforcing their weight and presence within the room and enhancing the overall sense of realism.

The final render highlights the ceramic vase and cups through carefully adjusted lighting, creating a more realistic depiction of their material qualities. Soft, directional light was used to produce subtle highlights and gentle reflections on the ceramic surfaces, helping to define their curvature and smooth finish.

The interaction between light and shadow enhances realism by showing slight variations in brightness across the objects, rather than a flat appearance. Reflections from the marble table further reinforce the material contrast, allowing the ceramics to feel grounded within the scene and visually integrated into the overall lighting environment.

The Pac-Man arcade set was modelled from scratch in Blender and designed to act as a focal point within the TV room. Its recognizable shape and scale help anchor the scene, while the proportions were carefully adjusted to maintain realism and visual balance within the environment. The cabinet was constructed using clean geometry, with attention given to sharp edges, panel separation, and overall structure.

The Nintendo Playboy was modelled and placed on the leather sofa to add character and a sense of personality to the room. Its positioning was carefully considered, resting at a slight angle on the cushions to avoid a rigid or artificial look. This placement helps suggest casual use, reinforcing the idea of the space being lived in rather than staged.

Material contrast was an important factor in this detail. The smooth, flat surface of the Nintendo Playboy contrasts with the textured leather material of the sofa, while subtle shadows and surface contact help ground the object realistically. Lighting was used to softly highlight the edges and surface, allowing it to integrate naturally into the scene while contributing to the overall narrative and realism of the environment.

This render focuses on the Nintendo Playboy with an illuminated green screen to enhance realism and visual interest. Emissive lighting was applied to the screen to simulate a powered-on device, casting a subtle green glow onto the surrounding surface of the console and the leather sofa beneath it.

The Nintendo Switch and Rubik's Cube were placed on the marble table to introduce variation in scale, color, and material. Their placement was carefully balanced to avoid clutter, with slight spacing and angled positioning to create a natural, everyday arrangement rather than a perfectly aligned setup.

Material contrast plays a key role in this composition. The smooth, semi-matte surfaces of the Nintendo Switch contrast with the glossy, patterned finish of the Rubik's Cube, while both objects stand out against the reflective marble tabletop. Subtle reflections and shadows help ground the objects, reinforcing their weight and presence while contributing to a realistic and visually engaging scene.

Virual Field Trip Assignment

This virtual field trip assignment focused on recreating a well-known Dublin landmark using photogrammetry and 3D workflows. I selected Luke Kelly's head statue, a tribute to one of Ireland's most influential folk musicians and a founding member of The Dubliners. Luke Kelly is remembered for his powerful voice, political activism, and lasting impact on Irish music and culture.

Using Meshroom, the statue was reconstructed from photographic data to generate a detailed 3D scan. The model was then cleaned and refined in Blender, where issues such as mesh errors, noise, and surface imperfections were corrected. Finally, the finished model was uploaded to Sketchfab to create an interactive, web-based 3D experience, allowing the piece to be explored digitally while preserving an important part of Dublin’s cultural heritage.

Luke Kelly's Head

Sketchfab Render

The Sketchfab render presents the final Luke Kelly head model as an interactive 3D asset, allowing viewers to explore the sculpture from any angle in real time. The platform’s lighting and shading tools were used to enhance surface detail and depth, showcasing the cleaned geometry and textures refined in Blender. This interactive presentation helps bridge physical heritage and digital preservation, making the landmark accessible in an engaging online format.

Unfortunately, due to time constraints, the material maps could not be consolidated into a single UV layout, so they did not transfer correctly to Sketchfab. As a result, only the base geometry and default shading are visible in the interactive model, rather than the fully textured version created in Blender.

After transferring the Luke Kelly model from Meshroom to Blender, the skin had numerous holes and some areas of hair were missing. These gaps were corrected by duplicating sections of hair textures and carefully readjusting parts of the head to restore the overall form. The skin and surface details were refined using Blender's healing, inflating, and smudging brushes, ensuring a smooth, realistic appearance while maintaining the integrity of the original scan.

The UV map revealed areas where the skin particles and segmented geometry were duplicated and merged to fill gaps in the model. These cloned sections had to be carefully reconstructed and adjusted to ensure continuity and natural flow, preserving the overall shape and realism of Luke Kelly's head.
