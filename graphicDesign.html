Graphic Design

I have developed strong graphic design skills through extensive hands-on experience using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and InDesign, which I use as my primary design tools for both academic and personal projects. My design approach is grounded in visual storytelling, where careful attention is given to composition, hierarchy, color, and typography to ensure each piece communicates its intended message clearly and effectively to the audience. I also place a strong emphasis on conceptual thinking, experimenting with shapes, textures, and layering to create depth and visual interest, often drawing inspiration from branding, editorial, and contemporary illustration styles.

Design has always been a significant influence on my creative process, shaping how I approach problem-solving, concept development, and layout decisions. While I recognise that there is still much to learn, I am highly motivated to continue developing my skills, experimenting with new techniques, and refining my creative thinking when it comes to visual communication. I am eager to grow as a designer and constantly challenge myself to improve, using each project as a new opportunity to become more confident, inventive, and technically proficient.

Killiney Wine Poster

illustrator

This poster was created for the Killiney Golf Club event, Taste of Killiney, using Adobe Illustrator. The bottle and wine elements were designed entirely from shapes, while the fruit was generated with AI to add visual interest. The color palette and overall design align with the Killiney Golf Club's branding, ensuring a cohesive and professional look. Informational bubbles were incorporated to present event details clearly and hierarchically, guiding the viewer's eye while maintaining a visually engaging layout. This project allowed me to combine vector illustration, AI-generated elements, and brand-focused design in a cohesive composition.

Album Vinly Cover

Photoshop, Indesign & illustrator

This album cover was created for a four-member band, incorporating the album title (Design House), a hidden element, and all band members. The base layer features a duplicated image of Earth with a swirl effect, creating depth and movement, while three blindfolded members are positioned on the left and the fourth integrated seamlessly using color balance and filters.

Skeletons were added to symbolize Design House as a “never-ending music piece,” representing the enduring and cyclical nature of music and creativity. This adds a conceptual layer that complements the visual narrative.

The album title uses inflated 3D typography, and the hidden element—a teddy beside the title—is actually the letters “D & H” rotated horizontally. Overall, the design combines layering, blending, and storytelling to create a cohesive and imaginative album cover.

Movie Poster

Photoshop

This poster depicts a young boy in silhouette walking into the distance, with a dramatic sunset behind him, while above, a soldier holding a gun gazes into the horizon. Flying jets are layered subtly across the composition, and event details are displayed at the bottom to indicate the movie's release. The design is inspired by the Saving Private Ryan poster and explores themes of the American Dream during the Vietnam War.

For this assignment, only one AI-generated element was allowed, so I added a bag on the boy's right hand. The original image did not include a bag, so I used AI to generate it in the selected area, integrating it seamlessly with the figure.

The poster combines silhouette, layering, and subtle AI manipulation to create a cinematic, evocative image. By balancing visual storytelling with historical and thematic references, it communicates both mood and narrative while maintaining a cohesive and engaging composition.

Logo Design Graphic

illustrator

This logo was created for O'Brien Wines as part of a UCD assignment, integrating a wine bottle (middle section), wine glass(on the right), and spirit bottle (on the left) into a single cohesive design. The wine glass and spirit bottle were designed separately, then carefully subtracted from the sides of the wine bottle to create a unified composition.

The final design aligns with O'Brien Wines' branding guidelines, using a colour palette consistent with their identity. The project allowed me to explore vector-based construction, precise layering, and creative integration of multiple elements, resulting in a clean and visually appealing logo.
